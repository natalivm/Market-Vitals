The read · money flow

The whole institutional cycle, on one chart

NBIS didn’t just go up and come down — it walked through every stage of the flow model. Reading those stages is what tells you where you are before you place a trade. Right now we’re in the bounce off a flush, pushing back into overhead supply.

Stage 1 · Accumulation

The 49 base

The long, quiet shelf near 49.39 is where size was built — unfilled buy orders stacking with no urgency. Boring by design. That base is the launchpad for everything above it.

Stage 2 · Markup

The parabola to 353

Impulse after impulse — institutions marking price up through 100 → 199.90 → 222 → 281 to the 353.14 all-time high. Every dip got bought; the candles are big-bodied and one-directional. This is trend, not chop.

Stage 3 · Distribution

The 281–300 top

Weekly RSI printed ≈86 — climactic. Big upper wicks and heavy red candles at the highs = smart money selling into euphoria while retail chased. Supply overwhelmed demand and the trend rolled.

Stage 4 · Flush → re-accumulation

The July stab to ~155–158

Price broke 222 and 199.90, then ran stops below the 166 shelf into the 158.32 daily band and toward the rising 149.12 200-day. Long lower wicks and a sharp V back to 188 = demand stepping in. That’s the liquidity grab, then re-accumulation.

Where we are right now

188.71, below the 199.90 broken-support-turned-resistance and the 211.20 fifty-day EMA. This is a counter-trend bounce inside a broken daily structure — buyers reclaimed the flush, but they’re walking straight into the supply that caused the drop. Good things happen at levels; nothing good happens chasing price into a wall. The daily trend does not flip up until 199.90 → 211 is reclaimed and held.

Takeaway The parabola’s higher-timeframe uptrend is intact above 149. Everything between here and 222 is a supply repair job — trade the levels, not the excitement.
Today’s trade · the plan

Building the trade for today’s price action

Price is 188.71, bouncing into resistance. The framework answer is not “buy the bounce” — it’s two levels and two tickets. The long waits for a reaction at demand (166–178) or a confirmed reclaim of 199.90. The short is actually the trend-aligned trade right now: sell the rejection where this bounce runs into the 199.90–211 supply it broke. Scenarios first, then both tickets.

A · Buy the pullback, not the wall preferred

Let the bounce cool back into the 166–178 reclaimed shelf (hourly demand / BB-lower 166.61). That’s where re-accumulation buyers defend. Long the reaction, not the level itself.

Trigger: bullish impulse candle off 166–178 holding > 166 on volume

Targets 199.90 → 211.20 → 222.43. Stop below 163.

B · The A+ long — deep demand

If the bounce fails and 166 breaks, the 155–158 flush shelf (daily BB-lower 158.32) sitting just above the rising 149.12 200-day EMA is the highest-quality long on the map — buying re-accumulation at the trend spine.

Trigger: reclaim + impulse candle off 155–158 on volume

Targets 178 → 199.90 → 222. Stop below 149.

C · Short the wall trend-aligned

The daily is broken — below the 50-EMA, below 199.90. Selling this bounce as it stalls into the 199.90–211 supply (broken support + 50-day EMA 211.20 + 200-hour EMA 206.73) trades with the daily downtrend. Short the rejection, not a guess.

Trigger: bearish rejection / failed reclaim at 199.90–211 on volume

Targets 178.49 → 166.61 → 158.32. Stop above 213.

Invalidation · when “correction” becomes “trend change”

A decisive daily close below 149.12 (200-day EMA) breaks the parabola’s uptrend structure. Below that, the air pocket down toward the 100 area / the 49 base opens up. That is the line in the sand for every long idea above.

Kill switch: daily close < 149.12

The two are mirror images

The short’s invalidation is the long’s confirmation: a reclaim-and-hold above 211 kills the short and flips the daily up. And the long’s deep target (158–166) is exactly where the short covers. Don’t run both at once — let price pick the level. Into 199–211 = the short is live; down at 166–178 or 155–158 = the long is live; at 188.71 in the middle = neither.
