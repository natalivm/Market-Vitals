The read · money flow

The whole institutional cycle, on one chart

NBIS didn’t just go up and come down — it walked through every stage of the flow model. Reading those stages is what tells you where you are before you place a trade. Right now the flush is over and price has settled into balance — a tight coil between 180 and 190.49. Balance is a two-sided market: you fade the edges, you don’t chase the middle.

Stage 1 · Accumulation

The 49 base

The long, quiet shelf near 49.39 is where size was built — unfilled buy orders stacking with no urgency. Boring by design. That base is the launchpad for everything above it.

Stage 2 · Markup

The parabola to 353

Impulse after impulse — institutions marking price up through 100 → 199.90 → 222 → 281 to the 353.14 all-time high. Every dip got bought; the candles are big-bodied and one-directional. This is trend, not chop.

Stage 3 · Distribution

The 281–300 top

Weekly RSI printed ≈86 — climactic. Big upper wicks and heavy red candles at the highs = smart money selling into euphoria while retail chased. Supply overwhelmed demand and the trend rolled.

Stage 4 · Flush → balance

The July flush, then the coil

Price broke 222 and 199.90 and ran stops down to a ~163–168 low (7/17). Demand stepped in, and since then it has balanced in a tight range — 7/20 closed 182.62, pinned between the 180 floor and the 190.49 wall. RSI ~47–51, MACD flat: two-sided, coiling for the next expansion.

Where we are right now

182.62, sitting in the middle of a 180 ↔ 190.49 balance range, still below the 199.90 broken support and the 205 two-hundred-hour EMA. The bigger picture is a counter-trend bounce inside a broken daily structure — but tactically, today is a range. Good things happen at the edges (180 or 190.49); nothing good happens at 182.62 in the middle. Direction only picks up on a break of 176 (down, trend resumes) or a reclaim of 190.49 → 199.90 (up).

Takeaway Balance = fade the edges with a tight stop. Long ~180 with a stop under 179; short ~190.49 with a stop over 192. The wide-stop swing trade is a different, lower-odds bet — save it for the break.
Today’s trade · one full trade

One full trade to the end — short, tight stop

One direction, held to target. The dominant flow is down — the parabola distributed, the daily structure is broken (price under 199.90 and the 205 two-hundred-hour EMA), and this bounce is a weak balance beneath resistance. The full move is the air pocket to the 200-day EMA (~147), where the correction naturally ends. And the tight stop is free: you short the one hard line the range keeps failing at — 190.49 — with the stop just above it. Precise entry = small risk; trend alignment = full target.

Why this stop is tight (and your last one wasn’t)

Your earlier short entered a zone (185–200) with the stop out at 213 — $13–28 of risk, because the invalidation was the far side of a wide area. This one does the opposite: it enters at the single line (190.49) and stops just past it (192) — ~$1.5 risk for the same ~43-point target. The target didn’t shrink; the risk did. That is the entire trick to a tight stop: let the level define it, not your account size or your hope.

How “to the end” actually works

You don’t sit through 29R with the original stop — you trail. The instant 176 breaks, move the stop to your entry (190.5): now it’s a risk-free trade running to 147. Scale a third off at 168 and 160, ride the last third to the 200-day with the stop trailing each prior level. That is how one tight-stop entry becomes a full-move trade: small fixed risk at the start, then house money the rest of the way. Below 147 is a different trade (trend change → 130/100) — don’t improvise it, re-plan it.
