Volatility · The Fear Gauge · 01 / 08
VIX — The Market's Insurance Premium
VIX is what traders pay for crash protection on the S&P 500. When everyone wants insurance at once, the premium spikes — so a high VIX literally means fear is expensive.
STEP 1
😟 Traders fear a drop
News, data, or a wobble spooks the market.
→
STEP 2
🛡 They buy put options
Puts = the right to sell, i.e. portfolio insurance.
→
STEP 3
💰 Premiums get expensive
Demand for protection bids option prices up.
→
RESULT
📈 VIX rises
VIX reads those prices as 30-day expected swing.

Volatility · The Bond Market's VIX · 02 / 08
MOVE — Stress in the Plumbing
MOVE is VIX's sibling for U.S. Treasuries. Interest rates are the denominator under every asset price, so rate turmoil eventually becomes stock turmoil. Watching both together is the real skill.
two gauges, one verdict
How it works

MOVE tracks the implied volatility of options on 2-, 5-, 10- and 30-year Treasuries. When bond traders brace for big rate moves — a Fed surprise, hot inflation, a fiscal shock — they buy Treasury options and MOVE climbs.

The divergence is the signal
  • Both calm → "double green," the cleanest backdrop for a rally. (Today.)
  • VIX calm, MOVE spiking → stress is brewing in rates first; stocks usually catch down.
  • VIX spiking, MOVE calm → equity-specific scare, not systemic; tends to recover faster.
  • Both above the line → systemic stress; de-risk everything.
Calm 73.58 June 4: 73.58 → 73.58 — perfectly flat. The rates market sent zero stress signals, giving equity bulls the all-clear on the rate front for the entire session.

Breadth · Advancers vs Decliners · 03 / 08
McClellan — The Breadth Tug-of-War
A market index can rise on just a few giants. The McClellan Oscillator ignores price and asks: are more stocks rising than falling — and is that gap accelerating?
The mechanic

It's the gap between a 19-day and 39-day average of (advancing − declining stocks). Because it compares fast vs slow averages, it measures the rate of change of breadth — momentum, not level. Positive = the gap is widening in favour of advancers.

What traders watch
  • Divergence: index hits new highs but McClellan rolls over → rally is thinning. Warning.
  • Zero-line cross (− to +) confirms a broad regime shift.
  • >+100 can stay overbought for weeks in strong bulls — don't blindly fade.
Rising +7.63 June 4: +9.93 → +7.63. The knot sits just right of center — advancers in control. Slight easing into the close is routine end-of-day profit-taking, not deterioration.

Breadth · Structural Health · 04 / 08
% Above the 200-Day — Counting the Healthy
The single best gauge of long-term structure. A stock above its 200-day average is in an uptrend. How many of them are? Above 50% means the bull has real breadth, not just a few heroes.
EACH DOT = 1% OF THE MARKET
55.9 above 200-day · uptrend 44.1 below · downtrend
Why 50% is the line in the sand

Above 50%, most stocks participate — the rally is broad and durable. Below 50% while the index makes new highs is the classic bearish divergence: a shrinking group of mega-caps is carrying a hollow market.

The ladder
  • >70% — bull market, broad participation.
  • 50–70% — healthy. (Today: 55.9%)
  • 30–50% — mixed; deterioration under the surface.
  • <20% — capitulation; historically a major bottom.

Trend beats level: a reading rising from 40% is more bullish than one falling from 70%.

Healthy 55.9% June 4: 56.1% → 55.9%, essentially flat. Over half the market sits above long-term support — a rock-solid floor even though the composite opened in Fear.

Sentiment · Hedging Demand · 05 / 08
Put/Call — Weighing Fear Against Greed
Puts are bets on (or insurance against) a fall; calls are bets on a rise. Their ratio is a balance scale of sentiment — and as a contrarian tool, the extremes matter most.
88 puts traded for every 100 calls
The sweet spot

A reading of 0.85–0.90 is the "healthy skepticism" zone — enough hedging to climb the wall of worry, not so much that panic has set in. It's the most fertile ground for a sustained rally.

The contrarian extremes
  • <0.70 — too many calls, rampant greed. Complacency → contrarian bearish.
  • 0.85–0.90 — balanced & healthy. (Today: 0.88)
  • >1.10 — heavy put-buying, peak fear. Often a contrarian buy.

A small uptick into the close (0.85 → 0.88) is prudent protection after a rally, not fear.

Neutral 0.88 June 4: 0.85 → 0.88. Sitting squarely in the healthy zone with a small protective uptick at the bell — exactly the behaviour you want to see late in a strong session.

Risk · Early-Warning Signal · 06 / 08
Hindenburg Omen — When the Market Splits
A rare warning that fires when an unusual number of stocks hit new 52-week highs AND new 52-week lows at the same time — the market pulling itself apart in opposite directions.
a market with no consensus
Why it matters

Normally you see either lots of highs or lots of lows. Both together means the market has fractured into haves and have-nots — an internal rupture that has preceded many sharp selloffs. All four conditions must trigger together, and clusters of 3+ in 30 days are the most reliable.

The 4 trigger conditions
✓ Highs & lows each >2.2%
✓ Index above its 50-day MA
✓ McClellan negative
✓ Cluster within 30 days

Only ~25% of single signals lead to real declines — context (a falling market) makes it far more dangerous.

Clear · No triggers June 4: Clear → Clear, no triggers in the 30-day window. The market's internals are cohesive — divergence tail-risk is absent, reinforcing the healthy McClellan and 55.9% breadth.

Flow · Following Institutions · 07 / 08
Smart Money Flow — Who Trades When
A simple, powerful idea: the open is emotional (retail and algos reacting to overnight news) while the close is informed (institutions positioning). SMFI accumulates the difference.
Reading the combination
  • SMFI ↑ + market ↑ → institutions buying the rally. Strongest confirmation.
  • SMFI ↓ + market ↑ → they're not chasing. Caution. (Today)
  • SMFI ↑ + market ↓ → quiet accumulation; reversal setup.
  • SMFI ↓ + market ↓ → distribution. Stay out.
Why it's only "amber" today

SMFI drifting from 51,185 → 50,820 means institutions are watching, not chasing. In an extended rally that's rational profit-taking — cautious, not alarming. Worth a 2–3 session watch to see if conviction returns.

Neutral 50,820 June 4: 51,185 → 50,820. The lone amber on an otherwise green board — institutions haven't committed capital to chase price yet. Not a red flag on its own, but the one to monitor.

Flow · The Most Important Signal · 08 / 08
Market Tide — Real Dollars, Real Time
Forget surveys. Market Tide tracks the actual dollars of options premium flowing into bullish bets vs bearish bets. Direction = the regime; size = the conviction. A mid-session flip is the highest-conviction signal on the board.
a $115M swing across zero
Why it leads everything else

No other indicator captures live committed capital. When institutions turn bullish they pay up for calls (or sell puts), pushing net premium flow positive. The moment it crosses from negative to positive intraday is the cleanest entry signal the dashboard produces.

The thresholds
  • >+$100M strong bullish conviction.
  • +$20M→+$100M confirmed buyers. (Today: +$43M)
  • ±$20M contested, no edge — wait.
  • <−$100M distribution; hedge.

Confirm the flip holds 30–60 min before acting — false crosses happen.

Positive · Flip June 4: negative all session (−$72M) then flipped to +$43M into the close — the day's pivotal event, and the single biggest driver of the composite's jump from 56 to 63.

How to read the stack

All three above 50% = the rally has participation across every horizon, not just a short bounce. When the short line (12-day) sits above the long line (50-day), momentum is accelerating in the near term — the bullish ordering.

Warning signs
  • Any line slipping below 50% while the index makes new highs → divergence.
  • 12-day dropping under the 50-day → near-term momentum fading first.
  • All three under 40% → momentum collapse; go defensive.

Structural Risk · Watch Separately · caveat
Concentration — When the Index Is One Sector
Every gauge can glow green while danger builds underneath. With 18% of the S&P 500 in semiconductors, "the market" is really a handful of chip giants — and the dashboard can't see that on its own.
a tower balanced on one leg
The trap

VIX is calm because Nvidia, TSMC and AMD are calm. Breadth holds because those names drag the averages up. But one bad chip earnings report can drop the whole index 3–5% overnight — regardless of what the other 480 stocks do.

The specific fault lines
  • Single earnings shock — NVDA/AMD miss hits 18% of the index at once.
  • Memory supercycle — HBM sold out through 2026; cycles turn violently at the peak (SanDisk +3,006% YTD).
  • Cap- vs equal-weight gap now exceeds the dot-com peak — record top-heaviness.
What to watch
  • SOX breaking its 50-day while the S&P holds → first crack.
  • NVDA·AMD·MU all red, VIX still low → quiet distribution underway.
Structural watch The rule: don't let a clean dashboard breed complacency. At 2-decade-high concentration, the vitals measure the health of a few mega-caps — not the market. Size positions accordingly.

Appendix · Case Study · Hindenburg cluster
When the Omen Clusters — What History Says
A single Hindenburg signal is mostly noise. The rare, reliable version is a cluster — 7 triggers inside 30 trading days. Here is how the S&P 500 behaved after every such cluster since 1970 (n = 14).
Horizon Median % Positive
1 day−0.2%46%
1 week+0.1%54%
1 month−1.4%46%
3 months+2.7%62%
6 months+4.1%83%
12 months+5.3%73%
forward S&P 500 return after a 7-in-30 cluster · since 1970 · 3M avg max loss −7.8%
Near-term caution, not a crash call

The first month skews negative — only 46% of clusters were higher at 1M (median −1.4%), and the average 3-month max loss was −7.8%. But by 6–12 months the market was higher 73–83% of the time (median +4–5%). A cluster front-loads the risk; it rarely defines the next year on its own.

The damage clustered around real tops

The painful cases sat at genuine cycle peaks — 2000, 2007 (−18.7% / 12M) and 2020 (−33% 3M max loss). The benign ones (2013, 2015, 2017) fired mid-trend and resolved higher. The difference was always confirmation: a falling tape and a negative McClellan.
